Recipe #3

  • Time: 350 degrees over for 8-9 min
  • Serves: 12 people
Ingredients Quantity
all-purpose flour 1 and 2/3 cups (208g)
baking powder 1/2 teaspoon
baking soda 1/4 teaspoon
salt 1/2 teaspoon
unsalted butter, softened to room temperature 1/2 cup (115g; 1 stick)
granulated sugar 1 cup (200g)
large egg, at room temperature 1
pure vanilla extract 2 teaspoons
whole milk (or buttermilk) 1 cup (240ml)

Frosting

Ingredients Quantity
unsalted butter, softened to room temperature 7 Tablespoons (100g)
confectioners' sugar 1 and 3/4 cups (210g)
heavy cream or milk 2-3 teaspoons
pure vanilla extract 1 teaspoon

Coating

Ingredients Quantity
candy melts or coating (or pure white chocolate)* 32 ounces
sprinkles

Making cake

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350 degree F

Step 2:Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a medium bowl.
Set aside. beat the butter and sugar together in a large bowl until creamed, about 2 minutes
Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat on high speed until combined

Step 3:With the mixer running on low speed, add the dry
ingredients and milk to the wet ingredients until combined. make sure to leave no lumps in the batter
and that the batter is a thick.

Step 4: Bake for 30-36 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean
then let the cake to cool completely in the pan set on a wire rack.

Step 5:With a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter on medium
speed until creamy, about 2 minutes. Add confectioners' sugar, heavy cream, and vanilla extract
with the mixer running on low. Increase to high speed and beat for 3 full minutes.

Step 6:Crumble the cooled cake into the bowl on top of the frosting. Make sure there are no large
lumps. Turn the mixer on low and beat the frosting and cake crumbles together until combined.

Step 7:Measure 1 Tablespoon of moist cake mixture and roll into a ball. Place balls on a lined
baking sheet. Refrigerate for 2 hours or freeze for 1 hour. Re-roll the chilled balls to smooth out
, if needed.Place back into the fridge as you'll only work with a couple at a time.

Step 8:Melt the coating in a 2-cup liquid measuring cup (best for dunking!).
you can use a double boiler or microwave.

step 9:Coat the cake balls: Remove only 2-3 cake balls from the refrigerator at a time
Dip a lollipop stick about 1/2 inch into the coating, then insert into the center or the cake ball.
Only push it about halfway through the cake ball. Dip the cake ball into the coating until it is
completely covered. Make sure the coating covers the base of the cake ball where it meets the
lollipop stick. Very gently tap the stick against the edge of the measuring cup
to allow excess coating to drop off. Decorate the top with sprinkles and place upright into a
styrofoam block or box (as explained above). Repeat with remaining cake balls, only working
with some out of the refrigerator at a time.The cake balls must be very cold when dipping!

step 10:Coating will set within an hour. Store cake pops in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
